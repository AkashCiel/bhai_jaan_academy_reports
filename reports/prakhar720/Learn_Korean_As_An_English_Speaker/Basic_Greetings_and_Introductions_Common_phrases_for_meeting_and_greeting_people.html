Understanding Greetings

Greetings are the first words exchanged between people and serve as a gateway to building relationships. In Korean culture, greetings are not just formalities; they reflect respect, politeness, and an acknowledgment of social hierarchy. The Korean language has specific expressions that vary based on context, formality, and the relationship between speakers.

Theoretical Foundations and Practical Implications:

Social Hierarchy in Korean Greetings

Korea has a deeply ingrained social hierarchy that influences language use. Age and status are significant factors in determining the level of politeness required in greetings. For instance, younger individuals will use more respectful language when addressing elders or superiors. Learning these nuances is vital for effective communication and integrating into Korean society.

Emotional Connection Through Language

Greetings and introductions are not merely about exchanging words; they are an expression of emotional connection. The way you greet someone can convey sincerity, warmth, and respect. As you practice these phrases, consider how they resonate with your own experiences and the feelings they evoke in social interactions.
