Common Misconceptions

    • Misconception: SOV structure is just a direct translation of SVO sentences.

    Clarification: While it may seem tempting to translate directly from English to Korean, the context and nuances of the language mean that adjustments are often needed in word choice and sentence flow.

    Misconception: The subject can be omitted in Korean sentences.

    Clarification: In casual conversation, especially when context is clear, subjects can be omitted. However, when learning, it's crucial to practice including them to solidify understanding.

    Misconception: All sentences must strictly follow the SOV order.

    • Clarification: While SOV is the basic structure, Korean allows for flexibility. For example, in certain contexts or with emphasis, other structures may be used.
