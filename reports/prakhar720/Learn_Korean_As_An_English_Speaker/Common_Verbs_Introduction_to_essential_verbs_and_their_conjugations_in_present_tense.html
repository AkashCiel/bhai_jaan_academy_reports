Common Misconceptions:

1. Verb Conjugation Complexity

One common misconception is that verb conjugation in Korean is overly complicated. While it may seem daunting at first, understanding the patterns in conjugation can simplify the process. Focusing on the endings based on the final vowel of the verb stem can make it easier to remember.

2. Using Informal vs. Formal Speech

Another misconception is that informal speech is always appropriate. In Korean culture, the level of politeness matters greatly. It's essential to be aware of your audience and choose the correct form based on the context.

3. Direct Translation of Verbs

New learners often try to translate verbs directly from English to Korean without considering context. For example, "to have" in English can translate to different verbs in Korean depending on what is being discussed (e.g., ÏûàÎã§ (itda) for possession).
