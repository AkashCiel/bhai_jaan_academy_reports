Key Concepts:

1. Understanding Comparisons

Comparison is a linguistic tool that allows us to evaluate two or more items based on specific characteristics. In Korean, comparisons can be made between nouns, adjectives, and verbs.

Types of Comparisons:

    • Equal Comparisons: Expressing that two items are the same in some way.
    • Comparative Comparisons: Indicating one item has more or less of a certain quality than another.
    • Superlative Comparisons: Describing one item as having the highest or lowest degree of a quality among a group.
