Key Concepts:

1. Understanding Tenses

Tenses are grammatical categories that indicate the time of an action or state of being. In Korean, as in many languages, tenses are crucial for conveying when something happens. The primary tenses are:

    • Present Tense: Describes actions occurring now.
    • Past Tense: Describes actions that have already occurred.
    • Future Tense: Describes actions that will occur.

Each tense has its own structure and conjugation rules that are vital for correct usage.
