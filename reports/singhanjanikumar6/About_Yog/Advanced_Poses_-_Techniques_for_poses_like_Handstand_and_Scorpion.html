Techniques for Handstand (Adho Mukha Vrksasana)

Step-by-Step Guide:

    • Preparation:
    • Start with a strong foundation in poses like Downward Dog and Dolphin Pose.

    Engage in shoulder-strengthening exercises (e.g., Plank, Chaturanga).

    Warm-Up:

    • Perform wrist stretches and shoulder openers to prepare your body.

    Incorporate core activation exercises like Boat Pose.

    Execution:

    • Position yourself facing a wall for support.
    • Kick up from Downward Dog or use a wall to assist.

    Focus on engaging your core and maintaining a straight line from your wrists to your feet.

    Alignment:

    • Ensure your shoulders are directly above your wrists.

    Keep your legs engaged and your body in a straight line.

    Breath:

    • Inhale deeply as you kick up and hold the pose.
    • Maintain a steady breath to remain balanced.

Common Pitfalls:

    • Overarching the back: Keep your core engaged to avoid sagging.
    • Misalignment: Ensure your shoulders stay above your wrists.

Practice Tips:

    • Use a wall for support as you build strength and confidence.
    • Gradually increase the duration of your hold.

Techniques for Scorpion (Vrschikasana)

Step-by-Step Guide:

    • Preparation:
    • Build strength in poses like Dolphin Pose and Forearm Stand.

    Incorporate backbends to enhance spinal flexibility.

    Warm-Up:

    • Engage in shoulder and chest openers.

    Practice core strengthening exercises.

    Execution:

    • Start in a forearm stand position against a wall.
    • Gradually bend your knees and draw them toward your chest while arching your back.

    Extend your legs toward the wall, resembling a scorpion's tail.

    Alignment:

    • Keep your hips over your shoulders.

    Maintain a strong core to support the lower back.

    Breath:

    • Inhale as you lift your legs and exhale as you hold the pose.
    • Focus on deep, steady breaths to maintain balance.

Common Pitfalls:

    • Lack of core engagement: This can lead to instability.
    • Overextending the back: Keep a neutral spine to avoid strain.

Practice Tips:

    • Use a wall for support until you feel confident.
    • Gradually increase the duration as you become stronger.
