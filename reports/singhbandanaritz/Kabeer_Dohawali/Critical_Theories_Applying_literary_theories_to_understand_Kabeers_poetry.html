Key Concepts and Definitions:

    Literary Theory: Literary theory refers to the systematic study of the nature of literature, its interpretation, and its relationship to society, history, and culture. It provides tools for analyzing texts and understanding their significance.

    Critical Theories: These are specific approaches within literary theory that focus on particular aspects of literature. They include Feminist Theory, Marxist Theory, Psychoanalytic Theory, Structuralism, Post-Structuralism, and more. Each theory offers a unique perspective on how to understand and interpret literary works.

    Contextual Analysis: This involves examining a literary work in relation to its historical, cultural, and social context. For Kabir, this would mean looking at the socio-political landscape of 15th-century India, including the Bhakti and Sufi movements that influenced his writing.

    Intertextuality: This concept refers to the relationship between texts and how they influence and reference each other. Kabir's poetry can be analyzed in relation to other works from his time, as well as contemporary literature.

    Reader-Response Theory: This theory asserts that the meaning of a text is created through the interaction between the reader and the text. Kabir's dohas resonate differently with each reader depending on their personal experiences and cultural background.
