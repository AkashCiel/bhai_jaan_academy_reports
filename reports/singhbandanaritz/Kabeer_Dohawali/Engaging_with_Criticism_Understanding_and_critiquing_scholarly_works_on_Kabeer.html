Key Concepts in Literary Criticism

To effectively critique scholarly works on Kabeer, we first need to familiarize ourselves with the fundamental concepts of literary criticism.

1. Definition of Literary Criticism

Literary criticism involves the analysis, interpretation, and evaluation of literary works. It can take various forms, including:

  • Formalism: Focuses on the text itself, examining structural elements such as form, style, and language.
  • Historical Criticism: Contextualizes a text within its historical and cultural setting.
  • Reader-Response Criticism: Emphasizes the reader's role in creating meaning from a text.
  • Marxist Criticism: Analyzes literature through the lens of class struggle and economic power dynamics.
  • Feminist Criticism: Explores gender dynamics and the representation of women in literature.
