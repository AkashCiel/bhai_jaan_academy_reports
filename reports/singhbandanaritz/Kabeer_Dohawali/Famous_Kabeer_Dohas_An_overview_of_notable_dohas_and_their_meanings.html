Key Concepts:

Definition of a Doha:

A doha is a couplet consisting of two lines (often referred to as padas) that conveys a complete thought or teaching in a rhythmic and impactful manner. The typical structure of a doha includes:

  • Syllable Count: Most dohas consist of 24 syllables, with the first line having 13 syllables and the second line having 11 syllables.
  • Rhyme and Rhythm: The rhyme scheme and rhythmic quality are integral, making the verses memorable and suited for oral recitation.

Kabir's Philosophy:

Kabir's teachings encompass a variety of themes, primarily focusing on:

  • Spirituality: Exploring the nature of God, the self, and the universe.
  • Equality: Advocating for social justice and the dismantling of caste barriers.
  • Introspection: Encouraging self-reflection and personal growth.
