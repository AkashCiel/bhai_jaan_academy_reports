2. Bhakti and Sufism

Definition: These are two influential spiritual movements in India during Kabir's time. Bhakti emphasizes personal devotion to God, while Sufism focuses on the mystical aspects of Islam.

Relevance: Understanding these movements helps contextualize Kabir's critiques of organized religion and caste systems, which are often reflected in his dohas.

3. Core Terms in Kabeer Dohawali

A. Ram and Allah

    • Definition: Both terms refer to the divine, with "Ram" being a central figure in Hinduism and "Allah" in Islam.
    • Example: Kabir often uses both terms interchangeably to illustrate the universal nature of divinity.

B. Maya

    • Definition: The concept of illusion or the material world that distracts individuals from spiritual truths.
    • Example: Kabir critiques attachment to worldly possessions and status, urging readers to seek deeper truths beyond maya.

C. Prem (Love)

    • Definition: A fundamental theme in Kabir's poetry, representing divine love that transcends all barriers.
    • Relevance: The emphasis on love as a unifying force aligns with Kabir's messages against sectarianism.

D. Atma (Soul)

    • Definition: The eternal self or soul, central to many philosophical discussions in Kabir's dohas.
    • Example: Kabir often explores the relationship between the atma and the universal soul (Paramatma), advocating for self-realization.
