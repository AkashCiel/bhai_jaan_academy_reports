Key Concepts and Definitions

Understanding Conflict

Conflict can be defined as a disagreement or clash between individuals or groups that arise from differences in values, beliefs, needs, or interests. Conflicts can manifest in various forms, including verbal disputes, emotional tensions, and even physical confrontations. Understanding the nature of conflict is crucial for effective resolution.

Types of Conflict:

  • Interpersonal Conflict: Disputes between individuals, often arising from misunderstandings or differing perspectives.
  • Intrapersonal Conflict: Internal struggles within an individual, such as conflicting desires or values.
  • Group Conflict: Disagreements that occur within or between groups, often due to differing goals or competition for resources.

Karmyog Principles as Tools for Conflict Resolution

    • Selflessness: The practice of acting for the benefit of others without seeking personal gain.
    • Detachment from Results: Focusing on actions rather than outcomes alleviates anxiety and allows for more balanced responses.
    • Duty (Dharma): Fulfilling responsibilities ethically and conscientiously, which fosters trust and integrity in relationships.
    • Equanimity: Maintaining a balanced state of mind amidst success and failure, encouraging calmness in conflict situations.
    • Continuous Improvement: Striving for personal and professional growth through learning and self-reflection, essential for adapting to new challenges in conflict scenarios.
