Key Concepts

1. Definition of a Country

A country is a distinct territorial body or political entity that is recognized as an independent nation. Each country has defined borders, a government, and a population that identifies with that nation. Countries vary widely in size, culture, political systems, and economic structures.

Example:

    • Canada is a country located in North America. It is known for its vast landscapes, multicultural population, and a parliamentary democracy.

2. Definition of a Capital

A capital is the city or town that functions as the seat of government for a country or region. It typically houses government institutions and officials, making it the political nucleus of the nation. Capitals often symbolize the culture and history of the country they represent.

Example:

    • Ottawa is the capital of Canada, serving as the center of Canadian politics and home to important national institutions like Parliament and the official residences of the Prime Minister and Governor General.

Real-World Applications

International Relations

Capitals are central to diplomacy and international relations. They are often the sites of embassies, international negotiations, and political events.
